### Frame Language Theory (Durgadas)
Durgadas introduced **frame language** as systemic critique:
- **Frame 1**: Extractive, hierarchical, two-dimensional "flat land" thinking. Uses literal dictionary terms (e.g., "governance," "accountability") that assume upward hierarchical flow. Nonregenerative by default.
- **Frame 2**: Three-dimensional, conditional/situational. Degraded form of Frame 3 — required for setting Frame 3's conditions. Hides more complex problems than Frame 1 because of its dimensionality.
- **Frame 3**: Holistic systems thinking, the inherent totality. Only humans can hold it; no system or AI can. Reference: Bhagavad Gita Chapter 4 (typological breakdown). Frame 3 is a relationship to action itself, not to specific activities. Failure mode: detached "above it all" spiritual bypassing.

Implication for the toolkit: current documents use nonregenerative Frame 1 language to describe regenerative processes — sabotaging intent. AI can only operate in Frame 1/Frame 2; cannot help escape to Frame 3. Designing the decomposition Frame 3 → Frame 2 → Frame 1 properly leaves a "ladder back up" that practitioners can climb, but only if a Frame 3 person maintains the connection.

### CSIS Structural Integrity
CSIS is the "proof of coordination protocol" — studies failure modes across organizational structures, balancing prevention (avoiding hacks/power concentration) with generative capacity. Two structural elements flagged for toolkit integration:
- **Dunbar numbers** — more granular analysis than Rob Dunbar's, with specific size limits per function and working-group sizing for growth past thresholds
- **Six-directional responsibility model** — every role has responsibility in at least six directions (vs. traditional upward-only hierarchy); incorporates native/indigenous relationship perspectives
