### 1) What (2025 execution)
Regen Coordination operated as shared coordination infrastructure across multiple networks, with execution concentrated on funding facilitation, reporting standards, and cross-network governance operations.

**Headline evidence (draft):**
- Coordinated GG23-related rounds and localized variants with **$84,000 total matching funds across 2025 rounds** (global + local contexts documented in activities report).
- Coordinated broader round infrastructure with historical evidence of **$236,284 raised across major rounds** and **2.95x capital multiple** from sponsorship-to-total deployment.
- Ran regular council coordination operations (**13 meetings documented**) and maintained shared coordination infrastructure connecting ReFi DAO, Greenpill Network, and Celo Public Goods.
- Advanced impact reporting infrastructure via Common Approach/CIDS-aligned activity-output framing and Karma-linked reporting workflows.

### 2) Why (coordination need)
RC’s work responds to a recurring ecosystem problem: capital, governance, tooling, and on-the-ground delivery remain structurally disconnected. Coordination infrastructure is needed to convert fragmented initiatives into compounding network outcomes.

Bread alignment matters because it grounds this coordination function in a governance-linked solidarity funding relationship, rather than ad-hoc coordination without accountability.

### 3) How (operating model)
- **Coordination layer**: council operations, partnership facilitation, shared hub/process maintenance.
- **Reporting layer**: activities + outputs + impact framing (with auditable documentation surfaces).
- **Funding/governance layer**: Bread Coop member-project relationship, participation in governance cycles, and coordination support tied to explicit operational scope.

### 4) Now (2026 direction)
2026 direction prioritizes:
1. Coordination infrastructure enhancement over net-new tool complexity.
2. Better network entanglement between local nodes, partner ecosystems, and funding/governance rails.
3. Stronger proof surfaces (website + reports + linked evidence) to support external credibility and funding opportunities.
