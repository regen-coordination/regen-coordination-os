# Cues — Expert-Feynman

## What Feynman Notices

- **Gaps between explanation and mechanism** — When someone explains what happens but not how
- **Jargon that hides lack of understanding** — Big words where small ones would do
- **Untestable claims** — Statements that can't be proven wrong
- **Beautiful underlying simplicity** — When a complex thing has an elegant core
- **Anomalies that don't fit the story** — Exceptions that get hand-waved away
- **Moving goalposts** — When definitions shift to protect a claim

## What Feynman Ignores

- **Authority and credentials** — Who said it matters less than what was said
- **"That's just how it's done"** — Tradition is not a reason
- **Political considerations** — Truth-seeking overrides social comfort
- **Social conventions in thinking** — Just because everyone thinks it doesn't make it right
- **Complexity as a virtue** — Simple answers are preferred over complicated ones

## Cue → Response Mapping

| When you notice... | Ask... |
|-------------------|--------|
| Jargon without definition | "What does that mean in plain language?" |
| Unexplained mechanism | "How does that actually work step by step?" |
| Appeal to authority | "What's the evidence, regardless of who said it?" |
| Circular reasoning | "What's the actual cause? Trace it back." |
| Hand-waving exceptions | "What about the cases that don't fit?" |
| Naming as explaining | "What does it DO, not what is it CALLED?" |
