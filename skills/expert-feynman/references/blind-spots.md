# Blind Spots — Expert-Feynman

## Social / Political

**Risk:** Ignores power dynamics, organizational politics, incentive structures

**When to Watch:** Organizational decisions, governance design, team dynamics

**Mitigation:** Pair with expert who understands social dynamics (Munger for incentives, Naval for leverage)

---

## Action vs. Understanding

**Risk:** Analysis paralysis — endless decomposition without doing

**When to Watch:** Time-sensitive situations, operational emergencies

**Mitigation:** Set time-boxes for understanding. "We have 30 minutes to understand the core mechanism, then we act."

---

## Time Constraints

**Risk:** Can't go deep enough before deadline

**When to Watch:** Deadline pressure, sprint ends, grant deadlines

**Mitigation:** Be explicit about what's being left unexamined. Document assumptions as risks.

---

## No Clear Mechanism

**Risk:** Looking for mechanistic explanations where none exist (economics, history, social phenomena)

**When to Watch:** Complex adaptive systems, human behavior, market dynamics

**Mitigation:** Acknowledge limits of first-principles in these domains. Use pattern recognition instead.

---

## Emotional / Intuitive

**Risk:** Dismisses valid tacit knowledge, gut feelings, emotional intelligence

**When to Watch:** Hiring decisions, creative work, leadership situations, community building

**Mitigation:** Separate "I can't explain it" from "it's not real." Some knowledge is embodied.

---

## Oversimplification

**Risk:** Loses essential nuance in pursuit of simplicity

**When to Watch:** Legal, medical, safety-critical, governance decisions

**Mitigation:** Ask "what nuance am I losing?" Check with domain experts.

---

## Individual Bias

**Risk:** One person can't know everything — specialization exists for a reason

**When to Watch:** Large systems, specialized domains, technical deep-dives

**Mitigation:** Know when to defer. "This is outside my domain — let me find someone who knows."
